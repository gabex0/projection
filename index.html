How to Use This Tool

Enter your current ages, account balances, return rates, and spending in the left sidebar. The model recalculates instantly with every keystroke.

The Spreadsheet tab shows a year-by-year projection table. The Presentation tab shows a client-ready slideshow with charts and analysis.

Click + New Scenario to add a second scenario tab. Each tab has its own independent set of inputs.

Double-click a tab name to rename it. Right-click or click the × to delete a scenario.

All active scenarios are overlaid on the chart simultaneously so you can compare them side by side.

Switch to Reverse mode in the top bar. Enter your desired Target Retirement Income instead of contributions.

The model calculates the required annual savings rate to sustain that income throughout retirement. The result appears at the top and updates the chart.

Click Copy Link. This saves all your scenario data to your browser's local storage and generates a short link with just your plan name (e.g. ?id=BlueMountain).

Anyone who opens that link on the same browser will see your data. To share across browsers, download the CSV or Excel file.

⚠️ Links rely on local storage — clearing your browser data will break saved links.

Select a theme from the dropdown in the Presentation tab nav bar. 10 themes are available — colors change throughout all slides instantly.

Click PPTX to download a PowerPoint file with the selected theme applied to all slides, including embedded charts.

Click the ⛶ button to enter Fullscreen mode — perfect for presenting directly from this page.

Time — Starting age, retirement age, and end age define the projection window.

Assets — Current balances in taxable (brokerage) and retirement (401k/IRA) accounts.

Growth — Annual return rates and inflation. These drive all compound growth calculations.

Cash Flow — Contributions during accumulation, and spending model during retirement. Four spending models are available.

Income Streams — Social Security, pension, and rental income reduce the amount drawn from your portfolio each year.

Taxes — Ordinary income rate applies to traditional retirement withdrawals. RMD tax rate applies to mandatory distributions starting at age 73. Capital gains rate applies to taxable account gains.
